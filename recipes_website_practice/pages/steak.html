Steak

Ingredients

  • 2 ribeye steaks (1 inch thick)
  • Salt and fresh ground pepper
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 spring fresh rosemary

Instructions

  1. Season the steaks generously with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. Heat the oil in a large cast-iron skillet over high heat until it just begins to smoke.
  3. Place the steaks in the skillet and sear for 3-4 minutes on each side until a brown crust forms.
  4. Reduce the heat to medium and add the butter, garlic, thyme, and rosemary to the skillet.
  5. Tilt the skillet and spoon the melted butter and herbs over the steaks for about 1-2 minutes, basting continuously.
  6. Check for doneness: Remove steaks from the skillet when they reach your desired doneness (135Â°F for medium-rare).
  7. Rest the steaks for 5 minutes on a cutting board to allow juices to redistribute.
  8. Slice and serve the steaks with the pan juices drizzled on top.

Tips

  • Choose the right cut: Ribeye, strip, or filet mignon are great for pan-searing.
  • Let the steak rest before slicing to ensure it stays juicy.
  • Use a meat thermometer for perfect doneness every time.
